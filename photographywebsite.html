Biography

Collins Injera started his rugby debt while in high school at Vihiga Boys High School in Vihiga county.He played as a wing for the Kenya rugby cup while playing for ulinzi RFC, then Mwamba RFC.Injera debuted with the team at the 2006 Dubai Sevens and played in the 2009 Rugby World Cup Sevens, where Kenya reached the semifinals. He became the top try scorer for the 2008–09 IRB Sevens World Series season with 42 tries. He also scored 210 points and finished second behind Ben Gollings of England.In 2022, He was recalled into the Kenyan squad for the Rugby World Cup Sevens in Cape Town.He announced his retirement from rugby in January 2023.

Reviews
